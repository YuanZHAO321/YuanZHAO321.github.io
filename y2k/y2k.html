Research Projects
1. Oceanic heat loss and seafloor depth vs. crustal age (Year 2 tutorial, with Dr. Neil Mitchell, 2025–2026)
A data-driven scientific report investigating the relationship between oceanic heat loss and ocean basin morphology. Cross-section datasets were generated using GeoMapApp across the East Pacific Rise, Mid-Atlantic Ridge and Reykjanes Ridge. Subsidence models by Parsons & Sclater (1977), Parsons & McKenzie (1978), and Stein & Stein (1992) were tested against the observed depth-age relationship via log-linearisation. Result: Upper Second (2:1).

2. Titanium mine expansion: geological & environmental impact assessment (EART26001, Semester 1, 2025)
A consultant-style report on a simulated South African titanium placer mine expansion. Covers exploration techniques, a graphical market analysis of titanium supply chains and critical raw material status, and a colour-coded risk matrix assessing economic, environmental and societal impacts. Result: First Class.  NEW!

3. Acid rock drainage at Mam Tor (EART11300, Year 1, 2024)
Field-based scientific report assessing the extent to which acid rock drainage affects water quality around Mam Tor, Derbyshire. Field water sampling combined with stratigraphic context (Carboniferous shales and Coal Measures) to interpret pH, conductivity and metal ion distributions. Result: Upper Second (2:1).

4. Scotland advanced field course (EART25102, Spring 2026)
Fourteen days of field-based geological investigation in Scotland. Week 1 in Fort William focused on Caledonian metamorphic and structural geology; week 2 in Dundee covered Devonian sedimentary sequences and landscape evolution. Result: pending.
